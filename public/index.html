Housing Affordability

Median house prices are now 6 times the median income, up from 4-5 times 20 years ago. The ratio of median rents to median income rose from 25 to 30%.


The mortgage rate on a 30-year home loan soared from 3 ½ percent in early 2022 to nearly 8% in October 2023 as the Federal Reserve raised policy interest rates


Households — renters in particular — are increasingly cost-burdened. Among homeowners, about 40 percent of those in the $35,000-49,000 income range are cost-burdened.

Tax Reform

Tax reform can play a significant role in addressing housing affordability in the United States by influencing both supply and demand factors. Some ways it could help are:

  • Incentivizing Affordable Housing Development:
    Tax incentives for developers who build affordable housing units can significantly increase the supply of affordable housing, helping to lower prices and improve affordability for low- and moderate-income individuals and families.
  • Property Tax Policies:
    Reforming property tax policies, such as implementing exemptions or caps on property tax increases, can help protect long-term residents from displacement in gentrifying neighborhoods and alleviate financial burdens on homeowners, particularly those with low incomes.
  • Tax Credits for Low-Income Homebuyers:
    Providing tax credits or deductions for low-income individuals or families purchasing their first homes can help reduce the financial barrier to homeownership and promote wealth-building among disadvantaged communities.

Important Points

Adjust taxes in a way that encourages people who build affordable houses to make more of them and lose less money in their taxes.

Cap property tax increases to help alleviate financial stress on homeowners.

Give tax credits to individuals or families purchasing their first home to help people get into the market.

Housing Supply

We can advocate for the increase in supply of houses in the United States. Ways this can help are:

  • Meeting Demand:
    Increasing housing supply helps to meet the demand for housing, which can stabilize or reduce housing prices, benefiting both renters and potential homebuyers.
  • Alleviating Pressure on Low- and Moderate-Income Households:
    A larger supply of affordable housing options can provide more choices for low- and moderate-income households, helping to alleviate their housing cost burdens.
  • Preventing Gentrification and Displacement:
    Increasing housing supply in areas experiencing rapid development or gentrification can mitigate displacement by providing alternative housing options, preserving neighborhood diversity, and preventing the exclusion of low-income residents.

Important Points

Increasing the supply could reduce prices for buyers and renters.

It would allow for more options for low and moderate-income households to alleviate pressure.

It would help prevent a high homeless rate.

Budget Calculator Section

It is important to remember key factors to be aware of when looking into an increasingly costly housing market:

  • Down Payment: Saving for a down payment is essential, as it's typically required upfront and can significantly impact affordability.

  • Mortgage Payments: Calculating mortgage payments accurately is crucial to ensure they fit within your budget and include principal, interest, taxes, and insurance (PITI).

  • Property Taxes: Understanding property tax rates in the area you're buying and anticipating potential increases is important for budgeting purposes.

  • Homeowners Insurance: Obtaining insurance quotes and including the cost of homeowners insurance in your budget is essential to protect your home and belongings.

  • Private Mortgage Insurance (PMI): If your down payment is less than 20%, budgeting for PMI, which adds to your monthly mortgage payments, is necessary.

  • Home Maintenance and Repairs: Budgeting for ongoing maintenance and setting aside funds for unexpected repairs can prevent financial strain as a homeowner.

  • Utilities and Other Expenses: Accounting for utilities, HOA fees, landscaping, and furnishings in your budget is important for accurately estimating homeownership costs.

  • Emergency Fund: Building and maintaining an emergency fund with three to six months' worth of living expenses is crucial to cover unexpected homeownership expenses or financial setbacks.
